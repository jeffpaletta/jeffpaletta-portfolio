On paper I call myself a communication
designer, but that's really just an umbrella
term I use to keep things succinct. I'm
really just a problem solver who is always
looking for a better way of doing things.

As a kid I was building primitive “robots”
to do my chores for me, and as a teen I
was scheming up romantic creative
ways to ask my crush to the school
dance. Doing things the default way is
boring; there’s always a more exciting
means to accomplish the ends.

I’m basically a grown-up version of the
annoying little kid who responds to every
statement with “why?” But sometimes
asking “why?” turns into a “what if?” My
portfolio is a collection of these moments:
fifteen different solutions that began by
asking questions an exploring new ideas.

On paper I call myself a communication designer, but that's really just an umbrella term I use to keep things succinct.

Really I'm just a young designer who loves to solve problems and can tell great stories. I got into design because I was a talented artist who could make pretty work, but as my practice evolved I found that I was even better at making smart work.

Everything I make comes from an interest in making life more enjoyable. Whether that means making interfaces more intuitive for users, representing complex ideas and data in simplified, more comprehensive ways, or simply making something that's fun and can make you laugh.

My work is a product of constantly asking questions, challenging defaults, testing the limits, and exploring new and exciting ideas.
